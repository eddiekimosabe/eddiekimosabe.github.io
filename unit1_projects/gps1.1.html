Introduction to Pair Programming

Pair programming is a software development technique in which two programmers work together at one station, whether it be in person or remotely. One person, the driver, writes the code, while the other person, the navigator, reviews each line of code as it is typed. These two roles are switched between people depending on how they feel, what they planned, and a multitude of reasons. As the navigator reviews the code, he also considers the strategy and direction of the work. The navigator creates solutions and improvements for potential problems while the driver is free to code without having to worry about problems outside his assigned task. However, both programmers can dabble in one another’s territory depending on the workers’ chemistry and an understanding of one another’s work methods.

Importance of Pair Programming

Pair programming can help assist programmers become more productive and more knowledgeable at their jobs. By using the driver and navigator concept you are allowing two people to take up individual tasks, rather than having to multi-task. Additionally when you have someone working at the same workstation with you it will help you maintain focus in this concentration challenged age. Another wonderful thing about pair programming is that you will learn faster; this happens when you're either explaining why you're doing something the way you're doing it or by asking questions about why someone is solving the problem with a certain method.

Reflection

After Gunnari and I have had our guided pairing session we have both agreed to reflect and discuss on how we felt about pairing sessions. We’ve realized that we respect each other’s time so we end up focusing more and finishing the work faster than one would individually. We learned different programming techniques/styles from one another as there are numerous ways of saying the same thing. We both got along well and we met a new coding buddy. Overall, the pairing session was a positive learning experience.
